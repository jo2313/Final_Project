Tiktok

TikTok, whose mainland Chinese counterpart is Douyin, is a short-form video hosting service owned by ByteDance. It hosts user-submitted videos, which can range in duration from 3 seconds to 10 minutes. Since their launches, TikTok and Douyin have gained global popularity.

Tiktok's Algorithm Use

How?

Social platforms like TikTok originally kept their algorithms secret. This is because the algorithmic recommendation system is a proprietary technology that helps make each social network unique. But recently, as people have become more skeptical about algorithms, most social platforms have revealed the basic workings of their algorithms. TikTok's algorithmic ranking signals can be classified into three types.

User Interactions

Similar to Instagram's algorithm, Tiktok's algorithm analyzes content that users like or don't like. Additionally, the more you engage, the higher your video will rank and the more likely it will serve your target audience. For you pages recommend content based on several factors.

1. Likes
2. Saves
3. Shares
4. Comments
5. Favorites
6. Rewatches
7. Videos Created
8. Chosen to hide
9. Video Completion Rate
10. Marked as Not Interested
11. Reported as Inappropriate

Video Information

TikTok uses information about whether the video was created on Tiktok, caption information, and the details included in the video. Video information signals are based on the content users tend to seek out through Discover or Search. The platform builds on this trend and categorizes content based on users’ interests.

1. Effects
2. Sounds
2. Captions
3. Hashtags
5. Keywords
6. Treding topics

Device & Account settings (Location & Language)

TikTok's algorithm determines location and language through the user's device settings. They provide videos tailored to users based on their location and language. However, it does not have the same impact as interactivity and video information.

1. Language preference
2. Type of Mobile device
3. Country setting & Posting location
(More likely to see content from people in your own country)

What’s not included in the TikTok algorithm

TikTok's algorithm does not provide certain content to users.

1. Duplicated content
2. Content you’ve already seen
3. Content that is graphic or threatens the safety of users
4. Content the algorithm flags as spam (including videos “seeking to artificially increase traffic”)
